# Employee Handbook — Acme Innovations Pvt. Ltd.
## FAQ Format (20 Questions)

---

### Section 1: Welcome Mission & Core Values

**Q1. What is Acme Innovations' core mission and how does it shape company culture?**
Our mission is to build technology that empowers people while operating with unwavering integrity in everything we do. Our culture is built on four pillars: transparency, ownership, collaboration, and continuous learning, and every employee — from interns to senior leadership — is expected to embody these values daily. We believe that a fresher's first ninety days set the tone for their entire career here, so we invest heavily in mentorship, structured feedback loops, and open-door policies. Ultimately, we hold every team member accountable to the same ethical standard: do what is right for the customer and the company, even when no one is watching.

**Q2. What does "operational transparency" actually mean in practice at Acme?**
Operational transparency means that key decisions, project statuses, and company performance metrics are shared openly across teams through our internal dashboards, rather than being siloed within management layers. This includes quarterly all-hands meetings where leadership shares financial health, hiring plans, and strategic pivots directly with the entire company. Employees are encouraged to ask questions during these sessions, and a recording is always made available afterward for those who could not attend live. This openness is meant to build trust and ensure no one feels decisions are being made "behind closed doors."

**Q3. What happens during a new fresher's first ninety days?**
The first ninety days are treated as a critical onboarding and integration window, during which freshers are paired with a dedicated mentor from their team. Structured check-ins occur weekly for the first month and then bi-weekly for the remaining two months, focusing on skill development, cultural integration, and early wins. Managers are required to provide written feedback at the 30, 60, and 90-day marks through the HR Portal. This structured cadence ensures no fresher feels lost or unsupported during their earliest and most formative weeks.

**Q4. Who is expected to uphold the company's core values — is it just for leadership?**
No — the four pillars of transparency, ownership, collaboration, and continuous learning apply equally to every employee, regardless of title or tenure. Interns and freshers are held to the same ethical standard as senior leadership, since the company believes culture is built bottom-up as much as top-down. Ownership specifically means taking responsibility for outcomes rather than just tasks, even when things don't go as planned. Continuous learning is reinforced through mandatory quarterly learning hours that every employee, including executives, must log.

**Q5. What is the underlying ethical standard employees are expected to follow?**
The company's guiding ethical principle is simple: do what is right for the customer and the company, even when no one is watching. This means employees are trusted to make good judgment calls in ambiguous situations without needing explicit rules for every scenario. It also underpins our approach to expense reporting, client commitments, and internal collaboration — honesty is valued over optics. Violations of this trust, even in small ways, are taken seriously during performance reviews.

### Section 2: Working Hours & Attendance Tracking

**Q6. What are the standard working hours at Acme Innovations?**
Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a mandatory one-hour lunch break. Employees can flex their lunch break anytime between 12:30 PM and 2:00 PM based on team coordination needs. This flexibility is meant to accommodate different team meeting schedules while still ensuring a full workday is completed. Core collaboration hours are considered to be 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM, when most meetings are scheduled.

**Q7. How exactly does attendance tracking work?**
All attendance is tracked digitally through the Acme HR Portal, accessible at hr.acmeinnovations.internal. Employees must clock in upon arrival and clock out at the end of the day using either the desktop widget or the mobile app's geofenced check-in feature. The geofencing ensures check-ins are only accepted when the employee is physically within office premises or an approved WFH location. Failure to clock in or out on a given day results in that day being marked "incomplete" unless manually corrected by a manager.

**Q8. What counts as a "late login" and what happens if I'm late?**
The system automatically flags any login recorded after 9:15 AM as a late login, giving employees a small 15-minute grace window. Employees accumulating more than three late logins in a rolling 30-day window will receive an automated notification, followed by a formal check-in conversation with their reporting manager. This isn't meant to be punitive on the first offense — it's primarily a pattern-detection mechanism. Persistent late logins beyond the initial conversation may be documented in the employee's performance file.

**Q9. What should I do if I know I'll be late in advance?**
Employees who anticipate a late arrival are expected to proactively message their manager and update their status on the HR Portal before 9:00 AM to avoid the automatic late flag. This proactive communication is generally viewed favorably and does not count against the rolling late-login count if done consistently. Simply clocking in late without any communication is what triggers concern, not occasional lateness itself. Managers have discretion to manually excuse a flagged login if proper notice was given.

**Q10. Does forgetting to clock out affect anything?**
Yes — repeated unexplained absences from clocking out are treated as incomplete attendance days and may affect eligibility for the monthly perfect-attendance recognition bonus. It also creates gaps in the automated payroll reconciliation system, which can delay processing if not corrected. Employees who forget to clock out should immediately message HR or their manager to have the record manually adjusted. This should ideally happen on the same day to avoid compounding errors across the pay cycle.

### Section 3: Work From Home (WFH) & Remote Allocation

**Q11. Can new freshers work from home immediately?**
No — remote work is a privilege extended only after an employee successfully completes their initial 30-day onboarding milestone. During this first month, full in-office presence is mandatory to ensure proper integration, tool access setup, and face-to-face mentorship. This in-person period is considered essential for building working relationships with the immediate team. Requesting WFH before this milestone is completed will be automatically declined by the HR Portal.

**Q12. How many WFH days am I allowed once eligible?**
Once the 30-day milestone is cleared, employees are permitted to work from home strictly two days per week. These days cannot be carried over or accumulated if unused in a given week — they simply reset. This cap applies uniformly across all departments unless a special accommodation has been approved by HR for documented medical reasons. Exceeding the two-day limit without approval will be flagged in the attendance system.

**Q13. How do I actually request a WFH day?**
Every WFH day must be formally requested and approved in advance through the HR Portal's "Remote Allocation" module. The reporting manager reviews the request against team coverage needs, upcoming deadlines, and meeting schedules before granting approval. Requests are generally expected at least one business day in advance except in emergencies. Same-day WFH requests are handled at the manager's sole discretion and are not guaranteed.

**Q14. Am I expected to work the same way on a WFH day as in the office?**
Yes — employees are expected to remain fully reachable during core working hours on WFH days, with the same responsiveness expected as an in-office day. This includes availability for video calls, Slack communications, and any scheduled meetings. There is no reduction in expected output or availability simply because you are working remotely. Managers may occasionally schedule a quick video check-in on WFH days to confirm availability.

**Q15. Can my WFH privileges be revoked?**
Yes — any pattern of WFH requests that conflicts with critical sprint deadlines or client-facing meetings may be declined at the manager's discretion. More seriously, habitual last-minute WFH requests without proper justification may result in a temporary suspension of remote privileges altogether. This is treated as a coaching conversation first, with suspension only occurring after repeated disregard for the process. Privileges are typically reinstated after a demonstrated period of proper planning.

### Section 4: Leave Policies & Medical Claims

**Q16. How many casual and earned leave days do I get per year?**
Every employee is entitled to 12 casual leave days and 15 earned leave days annually. Casual leave is intended for short-notice personal matters, while earned leave accrues monthly and is meant for planned time off such as vacations or family events. Earned leave accrues at a rate of roughly 1.25 days per month worked. Unused earned leave beyond a certain cap may be eligible for encashment at year-end, per finance policy.

**Q17. How much notice do I need to give for leave requests?**
Casual leave requests must be submitted through the HR Portal at least 24 hours in advance where possible. Earned leave, since it's typically planned further ahead, requires a minimum of five working days' notice to allow for proper workload redistribution within the team. Emergency casual leave (e.g., sudden illness) can be applied for retroactively on the same day with manager notification. Repeated retroactive requests without genuine emergencies may be questioned during reviews.

**Q18. How do I file a medical reimbursement claim?**
Employees must log the claim under the "Medical Reimbursement" tab on the HR Portal within 30 days of the expense being incurred. You'll need to attach scanned copies of original prescriptions, diagnostic reports, and itemized hospital or pharmacy bills as valid supporting documentation. Claims submitted without proper documentation will not be processed until the missing items are provided. It's recommended to photograph receipts immediately at the time of purchase to avoid losing them.

**Q19. What is the verification process once I submit a medical claim?**
Claims undergo a two-stage verification process: first, an automated check for document completeness, followed by a manual review from the HR finance team. This full process typically takes 7 to 10 business days to complete before reimbursement is issued. You can track the status of your claim in real time through the "My Claims" dashboard on the HR Portal. Approved claims are reimbursed directly to your registered bank account in the next payroll cycle.

**Q20. What happens if my medical claim documents are incomplete or unreadable?**
Claims lacking original itemized bills or containing illegible scans will be returned to the employee for resubmission rather than being rejected outright. You'll receive an automated notification specifying exactly which document needs to be corrected or re-uploaded. It's strongly recommended to retain physical copies of all medical documentation until reimbursement is confirmed, in case additional verification is requested. Repeated resubmissions can extend the 7–10 business day processing window significantly.
